Interest received, by tax year

Only the interest column is taxable income to the lender; principal is a return of capital. Figures reflect payments actually recorded as received. Scheduled (not-yet-received) payments are excluded.

How the payment arrives

Payment ledger

Interest is charged first on each payment; the remainder reduces principal. A short payment leaves unpaid interest that carries to the next month. Late fees and default interest are informational only — they are never auto-applied to the balance.

Scheduled interest, if every payment is made on time

How this record works

The lender records exceptions — a missed, late, partial, or extra payment — in a single file, ledger.json. The amortization is recalculated from those actual amounts and dates, so a late or partial payment correctly changes how much of every later payment goes to interest versus principal.

Every change to this ledger is permanently recorded in the public commit history, with a timestamp and author. Nothing can be altered silently. Both parties can review the full history: view the change log →

If you believe an entry is wrong, email the lender. This ledger is maintained by the lender and is not a substitute for your own records or bank statements.
